CENTRAL ELECTRICITY AUTHORITY (CEA)
TECHNICAL STANDARDS FOR CONNECTIVITY OF DISTRIBUTED GENERATION RESOURCES
REGULATIONS 2023

CHAPTER 1 - GENERAL PROVISIONS

1.1 Short Title
These regulations shall be called the CEA (Technical Standards for Connectivity
of Distributed Generation Resources) Regulations, 2023.

1.2 Applicability
These regulations apply to all distributed generation resources including
rooftop solar, ground mount solar, wind energy, and battery energy storage
systems connected to the distribution network at voltage levels up to 33kV.

1.3 Definitions
- Distributed Generation (DG): Power generation facilities connected directly
  to the distribution network with capacity up to 10 MW.
- Point of Common Coupling (PCC): The point where the DG resource connects
  to the utility distribution network.
- Anti-islanding Protection: Protection mechanism to prevent DG from
  energising a de-energised section of the utility network.

CHAPTER 2 - CONNECTIVITY REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Voltage Level Connectivity
- Up to 10 kW        : 415V LT single phase or three phase
- 10 kW to 100 kW    : 415V LT three phase
- 100 kW to 2 MW     : 11kV HT connection
- 2 MW to 10 MW      : 33kV HT connection

2.2 Power Quality Requirements
- Voltage regulation  : Plus or minus 6 percent of nominal voltage at PCC
- Frequency range     : 47.5 Hz to 51.5 Hz for continuous operation
- Power factor        : Minimum 0.85 lagging to 0.90 leading at PCC
- Total Harmonic Distortion (THD): Maximum 5 percent at PCC
- DC injection        : Maximum 0.5 percent of rated output current

2.3 Protection Requirements
All DG resources must install the following protection:
- Over voltage protection    : Disconnect at 110 percent of nominal voltage
- Under voltage protection   : Disconnect at 85 percent of nominal voltage
- Over frequency protection  : Disconnect above 51.5 Hz
- Under frequency protection : Disconnect below 47.5 Hz
- Anti-islanding protection  : Mandatory for all DG resources
- Earth fault protection     : Mandatory for HT connected DG

CHAPTER 3 - ROOFTOP SOLAR SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Net Metering Eligibility
- Residential consumers    : Up to 10 kW
- Commercial consumers     : Up to 500 kW
- Industrial consumers     : Up to 1 MW
- Group net metering       : Up to 2 MW for housing societies

3.2 Feeder Capacity Limits
- Maximum DG penetration on any 11kV feeder: 40 percent of peak load
- Maximum DG penetration on 415V LT network: 50 percent of transformer rating
- If DG exceeds 40 percent penetration, detailed load flow study mandatory

3.3 Interconnection Study Requirements
DG capacity above 500 kW on a single feeder requires:
- Short circuit level study
- Voltage rise assessment
- Protection coordination study
- Power quality impact assessment

CHAPTER 4 - EV CHARGING INFRASTRUCTURE

4.1 EV Charging Load Classification
- Level 1 AC charging  : Up to 3.3 kW, 415V single phase
- Level 2 AC charging  : 3.3 kW to 22 kW, 415V three phase
- Level 3 DC fast charging : 22 kW to 150 kW, 11kV or 415V
- Ultra-fast DC charging : Above 150 kW, dedicated 11kV feeder required

4.2 Grid Impact Assessment for EV
- EV charging load above 100 kW on LT network requires transformer upgrade study
- EV charging load above 500 kW on 11kV feeder requires power flow study
- Harmonic filter mandatory for DC fast chargers above 50 kW

CHAPTER 5 - METERING AND MONITORING

5.1 Smart Meter Requirements
- All DG resources above 10 kW must install bi-directional smart meters
- Net energy metering accuracy class: 0.5S
- Data logging interval: 15 minutes
- Remote communication: DLMS/COSEM protocol mandatory

5.2 SCADA Integration
- DG resources above 1 MW must provide SCADA connectivity to DISCOM
- Real time data: Active power, reactive power, voltage, frequency
- Control capability: Remote on/off, power ramp rate control
