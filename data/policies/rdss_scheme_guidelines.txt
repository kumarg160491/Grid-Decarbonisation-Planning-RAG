REVAMPED DISTRIBUTION SECTOR SCHEME (RDSS)
MINISTRY OF POWER - IMPLEMENTATION GUIDELINES 2022

1. SCHEME OVERVIEW

1.1 Objective
The Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme (RDSS) aims to improve the operational
and financial health of DISCOMs by providing result linked financial assistance
for infrastructure upgrades, smart metering, and system modernisation.

1.2 Total Outlay
- Central government grant component: Rs 97,631 crore
- Loan component through PFC and REC: Rs 1,51,000 crore
- State DISCOM contribution: 10 to 25 percent depending on state category

2. ELIGIBLE WORKS

2.1 Infrastructure Works
- Separation of agricultural feeders from domestic feeders
- Upgradation of rural 33kV and 11kV substations
- Distribution transformer replacement with higher capacity units
- Underground cabling in urban areas to reduce outage duration
- High Voltage Distribution System (HVDS) implementation

2.2 Smart Metering Works
- Prepaid smart meters for all consumers including LT and HT
- Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) communication network
- Data concentration units and meter data management system
- Integration with billing and customer care systems

2.3 IT and OT Infrastructure
- SCADA and DMS for 33kV and above substations
- Geographic Information System (GIS) for distribution network
- Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) integration
- Cyber security infrastructure for OT networks

3. RESULT LINKED DISBURSEMENT

3.1 Baseline Metrics (Must achieve to unlock grants)
- AT&C loss reduction of minimum 3 percent per year
- Collection efficiency above 98 percent
- Feeder level energy accounting operational
- 100 percent billing based on metered consumption

3.2 Performance Incentives
- AT&C loss below 12 percent: Additional 2 percent grant on project cost
- Collection efficiency above 99 percent: Additional 1 percent grant
- 24x7 power supply achievement: Additional 3 percent grant

4. SMART METER SPECIFICATIONS

4.1 Mandatory Features
- Bi-directional energy measurement (import and export)
- Time of Use (ToU) measurement capability (minimum 8 time slots)
- Power quality parameters: Voltage, current, frequency, power factor
- Event logging: Tamper events, power failure, load limit
- Communication: RF mesh or GPRS or NB-IoT (utility choice)
- Accuracy class 1.0 for residential, 0.5S for commercial

4.2 Data Communication Requirements
- Meter reading push interval: 15 minutes to head end system
- Command response time: Within 30 seconds for disconnect and reconnect
- Data retention: Minimum 60 days of interval data in meter

5. DISCOM ELIGIBILITY AND CONDITIONS

5.1 Financial Health Conditions
- DISCOM must have approved tariff order for current year
- Outstanding regulatory assets must have approved recovery plan
- State government guarantee required for loan component
- Quarterly performance reporting to Ministry of Power mandatory

5.2 Technology Partner Selection
- Open competitive bidding for all procurements above Rs 10 crore
- Make in India preference: Minimum 50 percent local content for smart meters
- Interoperability requirement: Open standards for AMI and SCADA systems
