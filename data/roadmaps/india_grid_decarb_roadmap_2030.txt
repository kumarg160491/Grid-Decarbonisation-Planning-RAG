INDIA GRID DECARBONISATION ROADMAP 2023 TO 2030
MINISTRY OF POWER - NATIONAL ELECTRICITY PLAN

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

India targets 500 GW of non-fossil fuel based electricity capacity by 2030
as part of its NDC commitments under the Paris Agreement. The distribution
network must be fundamentally transformed to absorb this unprecedented scale
of renewable energy integration.

SECTION 1 - CURRENT GRID STATUS (2023)

1.1 Generation Mix
- Coal                     : 52 percent of installed capacity
- Renewable (Solar + Wind) : 31 percent of installed capacity
- Hydro                    : 11 percent of installed capacity
- Nuclear + Gas + Other    : 6 percent of installed capacity

1.2 Distribution Network Challenges
- Aggregate Technical and Commercial (AT&C) losses: National average 17.1 percent
- Distribution transformer failure rate: 8 percent annually
- Average power outage duration: 4.2 hours per day in rural areas
- Smart meter penetration: 12 percent of total connections

SECTION 2 - TARGETS 2030

2.1 Renewable Capacity Targets
- Solar (utility scale)  : 280 GW
- Solar (rooftop)        : 40 GW
- Wind (onshore)         : 140 GW
- Wind (offshore)        : 30 GW
- Hydro and pumped storage: 62 GW
- Battery Energy Storage : 50 GWh

2.2 EV Penetration Targets
- Two wheelers EV share  : 40 percent of new sales
- Four wheelers EV share : 30 percent of new sales
- Public charging stations: 2.9 million by 2030
- Estimated EV charging demand: 90 GW peak by 2030

2.3 Smart Grid Targets
- Smart meters           : 250 million by 2025
- Advanced Metering Infrastructure: 100 percent coverage by 2027
- Automated Distribution Management: 50 percent of substations by 2028
- SCADA coverage         : 100 percent of 33kV and above by 2026

SECTION 3 - DISTRIBUTION NETWORK UPGRADE REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Transformer Upgrades
- Estimated 2.5 lakh distribution transformers to be replaced or upgraded
- New transformers must be solar and EV ready (higher kVA rating)
- Smart transformers with monitoring capability preferred
- Target: Reduce DT failure rate to below 3 percent by 2030

3.2 Feeder Separation
- 1.5 lakh km of agricultural feeder separation required
- Dedicated solar feeders in high DG penetration areas
- Cost estimate: Rs 2.8 lakh crore for feeder infrastructure

3.3 Substation Augmentation
- 33/11kV substation capacity addition: 450 GVA by 2030
- New substations with digital protection and control
- GIS substations preferred in urban areas
- STRUXURE Grid compatible control systems

SECTION 4 - TECHNOLOGY ROADMAP

4.1 Advanced Distribution Management System (ADMS)
Phase 1 (2023-2025): Deploy ADMS in all state capital cities
Phase 2 (2025-2027): Extend to all district headquarters
Phase 3 (2027-2030): Full national coverage

4.2 Energy Storage Deployment
- Grid scale BESS at 33kV substations: 10 GWh by 2026
- Distribution level BESS at 11kV: 15 GWh by 2028
- Behind the meter storage: 25 GWh by 2030

4.3 Demand Response Programs
- Time of Use (ToU) tariffs: Mandatory for consumers above 100 kW by 2025
- Direct load control programs for agricultural feeders
- Virtual Power Plant aggregation: 10 GW capacity by 2030

SECTION 5 - INVESTMENT REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Total Investment
- Generation               : Rs 12 lakh crore
- Transmission             : Rs 2.4 lakh crore
- Distribution (T&D)       : Rs 3.1 lakh crore
- Smart grid technologies  : Rs 1.5 lakh crore
- EV charging infrastructure: Rs 0.8 lakh crore
- Total                    : Rs 19.8 lakh crore (approximately USD 240 billion)

5.2 Funding Sources
- Central government grants: 30 percent via RDSS scheme
- State DISCOM borrowings  : 40 percent
- Private sector investment: 30 percent via PPP models
